About

A researcher focused on human-centric 3D understanding

I am currently completing my undergraduate studies in Artificial Intelligence at Chongqing University. Beginning in September 2026, I will join Beijing Normal University for my M.S. study under the supervision of Hongwen Zhang. My long-term goal is to build learning systems that better understand humans, their motions, and their interactions with objects and surrounding environments from visual observations.

I am especially interested in research problems where strong geometry, practical model design, and clear empirical analysis all matter. That includes monocular or sparse-view 3D reconstruction, interaction-aware generative modeling, and robust human-centered perception pipelines that can scale beyond narrow benchmarks. My current interests span 3D computer vision, vision-centric generative modeling, and human interaction understanding. I am particularly drawn to hand-object reconstruction from monocular inputs, human-scene interaction, generalizable pose estimation, and 3D generation methods that benefit from stronger structural or layout priors. I like projects that start from a concrete bottleneck, build a solid baseline, and then push forward through both model design and careful failure analysis.
